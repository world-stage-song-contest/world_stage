What the columns mean
Votings
The number of times one of the subject's entries came up in a show this voter took part in. The same entry can count more than once if it appeared in several shows.
Non-blank
How many of those votings this voter actually gave points in, rather than leaving the subject off their ballot.
Max
How many votings this voter gave the subject the highest score on offer.
Given
The total points this voter awarded the subject, added up across every voting.
Expected
The points this voter would have given if they had voted like everyone else. This is the baseline the bias is measured against.
Bias
How strongly this voter favours or works against the subject, compared with that baseline. Deeper fields carry more weight, because a point is harder to earn when more entries are competing for it. A positive number means they backed the subject more than the crowd did, and a negative number means they backed them less. It is 100 × ln((g + 2) ÷ (e + 2)), where g and e are Given and Expected after each show is weighted by its field size (votable entries ÷ points they gave). The + 2 steadies small samples.
Ratio
A simpler take on the same idea, as a plain percentage above or below expected. It ignores field size, so it reads naturally but can look extreme when the expected total is small. It is Given ÷ Expected − 1.
z
How confident we can be that the bias is real rather than chance. The further it sits from zero, the stronger the evidence. It is (g − e) ÷ √v, where g and e are the weighted Given and Expected and v is the weighted variance — each show's squared weight times its expected points.
{% if subject_type == 'user' %}
Received
The total points the subject awarded this voter's entries — the mirror image of what the voter gave.
Deficit
The gap between what this voter gave the subject and what they got back. A positive number means the voter supported the subject more than the subject supported them.
Got any
How many votings the subject gave this voter's entries at least some points in.
Got max
How many votings the subject gave this voter's entries the highest score on offer.
