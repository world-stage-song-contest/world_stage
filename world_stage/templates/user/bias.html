What the columns mean
Votings
The number of times one of their entries came up in a show you voted in. The same entry can count more than once if it appeared in several shows.
Non-blank
How many of those votings you actually gave them points in, rather than leaving them off your ballot.
Max
How many votings you gave them the highest score on offer.
Given
The total points you awarded them, added up across every voting.
Expected
The points you would have given them if you had voted like everyone else. This is the baseline the bias is measured against.
Bias
How strongly your voting favours or works against them, compared with that baseline. Deeper fields carry more weight, because a point is harder to earn when more entries are competing for it. A positive number means you backed them more than the crowd did, and a negative number means you backed them less. It is 100 × ln((g + 2) ÷ (e + 2)), where g and e are Given and Expected after each show is weighted by its field size (votable entries ÷ points you gave). The + 2 steadies small samples.
Ratio
A simpler take on the same idea, as a plain percentage above or below expected. It ignores field size, so it reads naturally but can look extreme when the expected total is small. It is Given ÷ Expected − 1.
z
How confident we can be that the bias is real rather than chance. The further it sits from zero, the stronger the evidence. It is (g − e) ÷ √v, where g and e are the weighted Given and Expected and v is the weighted variance — each show's squared weight times its expected points.

Received
The total points they awarded your entries — the mirror image of what you gave them.
Deficit
The gap between what you gave them and what they gave back. A positive number means you supported them more than they supported you.
Got any
How many votings they gave your entries at least some points in.
Got max
How many votings they gave your entries the highest score on offer.
